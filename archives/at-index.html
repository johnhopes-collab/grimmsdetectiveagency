Aarne-Thompson Index of Fairy Tale Types

The Aarne-Thompson Index is a scholarly catalogue of folktale types, published in German by Finnish folklorist Antti Aarne (1910) and later expanded and translated into English by American professor Stith Thompson (1928). It classifies traditional tales according to recurring plots, motifs, and narrative patterns found across cultures. The Index is an essential tool for folklorists, used along with Thompson's Motif-Index of Folk-Literature. In the world of Agency Grimm, Matilda is in close contact with Professor Thompson and uses advance copies of his work. It is sometimes seen as "ATU Index" following Uther's 21st Century additions. Navigating the Index

The AT Index lists the basic plots or central motifs of a vast number of folktales from various cultures categorized by overall plot type, each given an AT Number.

The seven main categories are:

  • Animal Tales...1-299
  • Tales of Magic...300-749
  • Religious Tales...750-849
  • Realistic Tales...850-999
  • Tales of the Stupid Ogre (Giant, Devil)...1000-1199
  • Anecdotes and Jokes...1200-1999
  • Formula Tales...2000-2399
