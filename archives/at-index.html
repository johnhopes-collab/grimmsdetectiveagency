Aarne-Thompson Index of Fairy Tale Types

The Aarne-Thompson Index is a scholarly catalogue of folktale types, published in German by Finnish folklorist Antti Aarne (1910) and later expanded and translated into English by American professor Stith Thompson (1928). It classifies traditional tales according to recurring plots, motifs, and narrative patterns found across cultures. The Index is an essential tool for folklorists, used along with Thompson's Motif-Index of Folk-Literature. In the world of Agency Grimm, Matilda is in close contact with Professor Thompson and uses advance copies of his work. It is sometimes seen as "ATU Index" following Uther's 21st Century additions. Hover over any tale name to view its full description.

Notes on Notation

KHM = Kinder- und Hausmärchen (e.g. KHM 81 would mean the 81st tale in Grimms' Fairy Tales, according to the 1850 Edition). German or English titles of the Grimm tales given where appropriate.

KHM 62a = Appeared as the 62nd tale in the First Edition (1812), but was later redacted.

330A = Subtype A of Type 330

Navigating the Index

The AT Index lists the basic plots or central motifs of a vast number of folktales from various cultures categorized by overall plot type, each given an AT Number.

The seven main categories are:

Animal Tales1-299
Tales of Magic300-749
Religious Tales750-849
Realistic Tales850-999
Tales of the Stupid Ogre (Giant, Devil)1000-1199
Anecdotes and Jokes1200-1999
Formula Tales2000-2399
